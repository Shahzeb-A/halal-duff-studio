Guide
What everything does — and how to shape a track.

How it works

Paste a song link and press Convert. The app isolates the original vocal, then rebuilds the low end with a real frame drum (duff) — no melodic instruments, ever. You get a clean voice-and-duff version in your Library.

The tune sliders

Play a track, tweak these in Studio, then press Re-render (a few seconds — it reuses the stems). Each re-render is saved as a version.

  • Heaviness — how deep and heavy the duff's low end sits under the voice, the "water beat" thump. Turn up for a heavier, more physical beat.
  • Duff level — overall loudness of the frame drum against the vocal.
  • Sub depth — how low the sub reaches. Lower = deeper and rounder, higher = tighter and punchier.
  • Beat swell — how much the beat rises up in the song's heavier sections vs staying flat throughout.
  • Brightness — air and sparkle up top. Below 0 darkens the master, above 0 opens it up.
  • Vocal duck — how much the drum ducks out of the way of the voice. Higher keeps the vocal clear and forward.

Re-render vs Match closer

  • Re-render applies your slider settings and remakes the mix.
  • Match closer to the original searches the groove + balance to get the beat as close to the original's feel as the duff honestly can, then reports a beat-match breakdown (placement / feel / tone). It won't fake a gain it can't get.

Versions

Every render is snapshotted. Open a track in Now Playing and use the Versions list to A/B compare tunings by ear — each shows its heaviness/duff settings so you can pick your favorite.

Local vs RunPod GPU

By default everything runs on this Mac (free, ~10–15 min a song). Add a RunPod key in Settings and tick RunPod GPU for ~2 min a song. A fresh GPU pod is spun up per song and always torn down after — you're only billed for those minutes.

Why voice + duff only

The frame drum carries rhythm; the melody stays in the voice. No melodic instrument is ever synthesized or reintroduced — that boundary is built into the engine, by design.
